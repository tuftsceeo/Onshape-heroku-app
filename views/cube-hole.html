Onshape-

Cube with a hole Approaches


Overview of Steps


1) Sketch a 2"X 2" Square

2) Extrude Add a Face

3) Sketch a Circle with a Diameter of 1 in

4) Extrude Remove a Hole

Approach #1


We tried!

This is an example using RECTANGLE, CIRCLE, and EXTRUDE ADD/REMOVE.

Step 1: Sketch a 2" x 2" square

In a new sketch, draw a Center Point Rectangle at the origin by going to the tool bar on the top and then clicking on the drop down arrow to select the Center Point Rectangle tool.

Then click once to start sketching and then click again to stop.

** Note: Every time you draw a new sketch, Onshape prompts you to define a plane to draw the sketch on. It's like picking which side of a sheet of paper you will be drawing on. **

On the tool bar, we will use the Dimension tool to specify the lengths of the sides. Click on a side of the rectangle to adjust the length to 2in.

Do the same for the other side of the rectangle to create a square.

Step 2: Extrude Add a Face

To create a cube, we want to use the Extrude function, which can be found on the left side of the tool bar.

** Note: There are two ways you can use the Extrude function: **

1) Click Extrude while you are still in Sketch 1 (the sketch of the square from before) or

2) Click the check mark to finish Sketch 1, click Extrude, and then click on Sketch 1 to specify what you are extruding

To make the square into a cube, we want to Add an extrude. Since this is our first extrude to this sketch, we are creating a New extrude.

Change the depth of the extrude (or height of the cube) to 2in.

Step 3: Sketch a Circle with a Diameter of 1 in

To make a hole, we need to start with a new sketch and draw a circle, a tool which can be found in the sketch tool bar.

** Remember again that Onshape will always prompt you to define a plane where the sketch will be drawn, so click on any plane that you want to extrude a hole through. **

** For a better view, you can change your perspective view to the plane you chose to draw a sketch on. Ex: top view for sketching on the top plane **

Click on the circle icon in the tool bar and draw a Center Point Circle by clicking once to start the sketch and then clicking again to stop.

We can use the Dimension tool again to specify the length of the diameter by clicking on the rim of the circle (circumference). Set this to 1 in.

Step 4: Extrude Remove a Hole

To actually make a hole, we want to use the Extrude function again, but instead of adding an extrude, we want to remove an extrude.

In the same sketch, click the extrude tool and click remove.

Set the depth to 2in because we want the hole to go all the way through our cube (and remember that our cube is 2 inches tall).

** Note: You can only Extrude Remove a sketch if there already is an extruded part. In other words, you cannot extrude remove a surface. **

Approach #2


We tried!

This is an example using RECTANGLE, CIRCLE, LINE, EXTRUDE ADD, and SWEEP REMOVE.

Note: The first 3 steps of this approach are the exact same as those in Approach #1.

Step 1: Sketch a 2" x 2" Square

In a new sketch, draw a Center Point Rectangle at the origin by going to the tool bar on the top and then clicking on the drop down arrow to select the Center Point Rectangle tool.

Then click once to start sketching and then click again to stop.

** Note: Every time you draw a new sketch, Onshape prompts you to define a plane to draw the sketch on. It's like picking which side of a sheet of paper you will be drawing on. **

On the tool bar, we will use the Dimension tool to specify the lengths of the sides. Click on a side of the rectangle to adjust the length to 2in.

Do the same for the other side of the rectangle to create a square.

Step 2: Extrude Add a Face

To create a cube, we want to use the Extrude function, which can be found on the left side of the tool bar.

** Note: There are two ways you can use the Extrude function: **

1) Click Extrude while you are still in Sketch 1 (the sketch of the square from before) or

2) Click the check mark to finish Sketch 1, click Extrude, and then click on Sketch 1 to specify what you are extruding

To make the square into a cube, we want to Add an extrude. Since this is our first extrude to this sketch, we are creating a New extrude.

Change the depth of the extrude (or height of the cube) to 2in.

Step 3: Sketch a Circle with a Diameter of 1 in (radius of 0.5 inches)

To make a hole, we need to start with a new sketch and draw a circle, a tool which can be found in the sketch tool bar.

** Remember again that Onshape will always prompt you to define a plane where the sketch will be drawn, so click on any plane that you want to extrude a hole through. **

** For a better view, you can change your perspective view to the plane you chose to draw a sketch on. Ex: top view for sketching on the top plane **

Click on the circle icon in the tool bar and draw a Center Point Circle by clicking once to start the sketch and then clicking again to stop.

We can use the Dimension tool again to specify the length of the diameter by clicking on the rim of the circle (circumference). Set this to 1 in.

Step 4: Sketch a Line Path for the Hole

Another way to tell Onshape that you want a specific part of your piece empty is to specify the path in which a surface should follow through.

Since our cube and our circle on top are centered at the origin, we'll use the line tool to draw a line from the center of the circle that you just drew to the origin, which is there the bottom face of the cube lies.

To do this, select either the Front plane or the Right plane and then draw a line from the center point of the circle to the origin and click the green check mark to finish your sketch.

** Note: We chose either the Front plane or the Right plane because remember that whichever plane we choose is where our sketch will go. Therefore, we have to choose a plane that clearly intersects the center of the circle and the origin. For this reason, we cannot choose the Top plane since it doesn't go through the center points; our line would just be drawn on the bottom of our cube. **

Step 5: Cut Out a Hole in the Cube

Now that we have the shape of our hole (circle on the top plane) and a clear path for it (the line that goes through the cube from the top center to the origin, or the bottom center), we can use the sweep tool to cut out our hole.

Select the sweep tool and make sure you are under "Solid" instead of "Surface." We want to sweep the entire circle to create a solid hole and not just the surface of the circle.

Select the face of the circle on the top for the "Faces and sketch regions to sweep," then select "Sweep path" and click on the line going through the cube.

Onshape by default assumes that you are always Adding a feature (like sweep, extrude, etc.), so you'll probably receive an error at this point. Make sure to switch it to "Remove" instead of "Add" sweep because again, we want to remove the solid hole from the cube.

Click the green check mark to finish your sweep.
