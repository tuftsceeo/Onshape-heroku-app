Approach #1


We tried!

We used CIRCLE, LINE, SPLINE, MIRROR, and EXTRUDE.

Step 1: Use the Circle Tool to Create the Collar

Create the round shape of the collar by drawing two circles. To draw the outer circle, click on the circle tool with the new sketch still in editing mode. Click on the origin with the circle tool, and drag outwards until the circle has your desired diameter. Once the circle is the proper size, release your cursor, and the circle will be complete.

Draw the inner circle with its center at the origin again. The inner circle should have a slightly smaller diameter than the outer circle (about .5 inches less).

Step 2: Use the Extrude Tool to Make the Collar 3D

Begin a new extrusion by clicking the "extrude" icon in the toolbar. Then select the space between the inner and outer circle. This will automatically give your collar a depth of 1 inch. You can change the depth of the sketch in the menu that appears when you edit the extrusion.

Optional Step: Use the Appearance Panel to Change the Color of your Collar

Right click on the collar part and select "edit appearance."

Then choose the color of your liking using the Palette or Mixer.

-- New Part --

Step 3: Use the 3 Point Arc Tool to Create a Ring

Create a new part studio by clicking on the "+" icon at the bottom left corner of the screen. If you wish to name the part studios according to the parts they contain, you do this by right clicking on the part studio tabs. Pivot your screen to see the front plane head-on. Create a new sketch in the front plane.

Within the sketch, draw an arc by clicking where you'd like to place the starting point of the arc, the end of the arc, and the radius of the arc. The distance between the ends of the arc should be at least 0.5 inches since our collar is 0.5 inches tall.

Step 4: Use the 3 Point Arc Tool Again to Create Outer Part of Ring

Draw another arc that is slightly bigger than the first arc you drew.

Use the dimension tool to make sure that the distance between the first arc and the second arc are about 0.1 inches.

Step 5: Use the Line Tool to Close Off the Ring

Use lines to attach the outer arc to the inner arc.

Use the dimension tool to make sure that the thickness of the ring is still about 0.1 inches.

Step 6: Use the Extrude Tool

While still in Sketch mode, click on the extrude tool and extrude add to make your ring 3D.

This works because we closed off our ring with the lines; if we didn't close off our ring, we would not get a 3D ring.

Optional Step: Color your Ring

Use the Appearance Panel to give your ring some color.

-- New Part --

Step 7: Use the Line Tool to Start Creating your Tag

Start by creating a quarter of the tag.

Use the dimension tool to make sure that the distance from the line you drew to the center is 0.15 inches.

Depending on how long you want your tag to be, specify a length for the line segment. In this case, I set it to 0.2 inches.

Step 8: Use the Spline Tool to Shape the Tag

To get a tag that looks like a dog bone, use the spline tool to create the curves of the dog bone.

To have more control over how curved the bone is, click several times to create checkpoints and move in a circular path.

To make life simpler, only create one out of the four curves of the bone. This will make sense in the next step.

Step 9: Use the Mirror Tool to Finish the Tag Sketch

While still in sketch mode, click on the mirror tool to mirror your sketch, which represents a quarter of the collar, across the x and y axes.

** Note: You can choose which axis to mirror your sketch across first. Just remember that after you mirror it across one axis, you have to deselect the mirror tool and then select it again to be able to choose another axis.**

Step 10: Use the Extrude Tool to Finish the Tag

While still in sketch mode, select the extrude tool to make your tag 3D.

Step 11: Use the Circle Tool to Create a Hole for the Tag

Create a new sketch on the origin on top of one of the faces of the tag.

Use the circle tool to create a hole. Use the dimension tool and make the hole have a radius of 0.05 inches (diameter of 0.1 inches).

Step 12: Use the Extrude Tool Again for the Hole

While still in sketch mode, select the extrude tool to make your tag 3D.

To create the hole, you want to extrude remove.

Optional Step: Give your Tag Some Color

Use the appearance panel to change the color of your tag.
